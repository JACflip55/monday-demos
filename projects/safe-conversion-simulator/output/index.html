📚 SAFE Mechanics

What is a SAFE?

▶

A Simple Agreement for Future Equity is an investment contract that converts to equity when a priced round occurs. Unlike convertible notes, SAFEs have:

  • No interest rate
  • No maturity date
  • No debt on the balance sheet
  • Conversion only at a qualifying financing event

Valuation Cap

▶

The cap sets a maximum valuation at which your SAFE converts. If the Series A prices higher than your cap, you convert at the cap — getting more shares for the same investment.

→Example: $100K SAFE with $8M cap
→Series A at $12M pre-money
→You convert at $8M (not $12M) = 33% more shares

Discount Rate

▶

A discount gives you a percentage off the Series A price. A 20% discount means you pay 80 cents per share when new investors pay $1.

→Series A price: $1.50/share
→Your price: $1.50 × 0.80 = $1.20/share
→You get 25% more shares than new investors

Cap + Discount (takes the better)

▶

When a SAFE has both a cap AND a discount, the investor gets whichever produces more shares (lower effective price per share).

→Cap price: $100K ÷ ($8M ÷ 10M shares) = 125K shares
→Discount price: $100K ÷ ($1.20) = 83.3K shares
→Investor gets 125K shares (cap was better)

Pre-Money vs Post-Money SAFEs

▶

The 2018 Y Combinator post-money SAFE changed the game:

Pre-Money SAFEPost-Money SAFE
Cap includesOnly existing sharesAll SAFEs + options
Dilution clarityUncertain until roundKnown at signing
Multiple SAFEsDilute each other lessEach SAFE's % is fixed
Founder impactMore favorableMore dilutive

This simulator uses pre-money SAFE mechanics by default (more common outside YC). Toggle post-money mode in the settings below.

Pro-Rata Rights

▶

Pro-rata gives SAFE holders the right to invest in the priced round to maintain their ownership percentage. This is valuable because:

  • You can double down on winners
  • Prevents dilution in hot rounds
  • Most post-money SAFEs include pro-rata by default
  • Usually optional — you don't have to exercise it
