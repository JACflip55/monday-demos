## What I Built

A complete **Comprehensible Input Curriculum** designed specifically for parent-child language learning. This isn't a generic resource list — it's a structured progression built around:

1. **Krashen's Comprehensible Input theory** (Jack's preferred method)
2. **Hours-based milestones** (from Dreaming Spanish's research)
3. **Content that works for BOTH adult and child** watching together
4. **Visual + audio pairing** (Jack's preferred learning style)

---

## 🎯 The Core Principle

> "We acquire language when we understand messages." — Stephen Krashen

**No memorization. No grammar drills. No flashcards.**

Just watch content you understand ~95%+. Your brain does the rest. This is how every child learns their first language — and it works for adults too.

---

## 📊 The Roadmap: Hours to Fluency

Based on Dreaming Spanish's research across thousands of learners:

| Level | Hours | What You'll Understand |
|-------|-------|----------------------|
| **Level 1** | 0-50h | Superbeginner content, basic words |
| **Level 2** | 50-150h | Beginner content, simple stories |
| **Level 3** | 150-300h | Intermediate content, most YouTube |
| **Level 4** | 300-600h | Advanced content, TV shows |
| **Level 5** | 600-1000h | Native podcasts, news |
| **Level 6** | 1000-1500h | Near-native comprehension |
| **Level 7** | 1500h+ | Full fluency |

**Key insight:** Most people dramatically overestimate their hours. Track everything.

## 💡 Pro Tips

### For Maximum Acquisition:

1. **Track your hours religiously** — Dreaming Spanish has built-in tracking. Use it.
2. **Don't study, just watch** — No flashcards, no grammar tables. Trust the process.
3. **If you don't understand 80%+, go easier** — Comprehensible means comprehensible.
4. **Variety of speakers > one accent** — Mix Spain, Mexico, Colombia, Argentina.
5. **Songs count!** — Play Spanish music in the car.

### For Learning WITH Your Daughter:

1. **Make it a ritual** — "Saturday morning Spanish cartoons" becomes tradition.
2. **She'll probably learn faster** — Kids are input machines. Don't compare.
3. **Point at things and say the Spanish word** — TPR (Total Physical Response) works.
4. **Celebrate her output** — Even if yours is behind hers.
5. **No pressure** — If she's not feeling it one day, skip it.

### When You Hit a Wall:

1. **Drop down a level** — No shame. Consolidate before advancing.
2. **Try a different speaker/channel** — Fresh voices help.
3. **Take a break** — Your brain processes during sleep.
4. **Remember:** 1000 hours sounds like a lot, but it's just 30 min/day for 5 years — or 1 hour/day for 2.5 years.
