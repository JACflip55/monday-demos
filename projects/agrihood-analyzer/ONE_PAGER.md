# Agrihood Investment Thesis — One-Pager

## What Are Agrihoods?

Residential communities built around working farms. Think: farm-to-table living meets modern real estate development.

**Market Size**: 90+ in US (2026), 27+ planned, growing 15%+ annually

---

## Investment Opportunity

### Pricing Power
- **10-30% premium** vs traditional neighborhoods
- Serenbe (GA): +25% premium, $750k avg home
- Willowsford (VA): +20% premium, $720k avg home
- Buyers pay for sustainability + community + fresh food access

### Market Demand
- **73% of Americans** prioritize fresh food access (ULI survey)
- Millennials/Gen Z: sustainability > McMansions
- Remote work → escape cities without sacrificing amenities
- Limited supply (only 90 developments nationally)

### Recession Resilient
- Agritopia (2000), Serenbe (2004), Prairie Crossing (1994) all survived 2008
- Property values appreciated through downturn
- For-sale signs rare (NYT 2014)

### Government Support
- Faster zoning approvals (reduced time/cost)
- Tax incentives (property tax reductions, conservation easements)
- State/federal grants for ag preservation
- Political win (conservation + housing + local food)

## Investment Angles

### 1. Secondary Market Purchase
**Capital**: $150k-$200k (20-25% down)  
**Hold**: 3-5 years  
**Return**: 5-8% appreciation + optional rental income  
**Risk**: Low-Medium (established developments)

### 2. Early-Stage Development
**Capital**: $50k-$500k (LP stake)  
**Hold**: 5-7 years  
**Return**: 15-25% IRR  
**Risk**: High (construction, permitting, market timing)

### 3. Agtech/Services
**Capital**: $25k-$100k (startup)  
**Model**: Farm management software, CSA logistics, education programs  
**Market**: 90+ agrihoods nationally, recurring revenue  
**Risk**: Medium (execution, customer acquisition)

## Red Flags (Walk Away)

❌ Water rights unclear or temporary  
❌ No professional farm staff  
❌ HOA farm funding undefined  
❌ Developer has no agrihood experience  
❌ Location >60 miles from metro  
❌ Home prices <10% premium (value not resonating)

---

## Green Flags (Strong Buy Signals)

✅ Professional farm staff hired  
✅ Water rights secured permanently  
✅ HOA funds farm operations  
✅ 15-30% price premium  
✅ Days on market <45  
✅ Tax incentives + zoning advantages  
✅ LEED/organic certifications  
✅ <30 miles from major metro

## Due Diligence Checklist

**Market**
- [ ] Pull 12 months of sales comps
- [ ] Interview 3+ current residents
- [ ] Check days on market trend
- [ ] Verify price premium vs area

**Farm Operations**
- [ ] Confirm water rights (permanent, not annual permits)
- [ ] Meet farm manager / staff
- [ ] Review farm budget (HOA allocation)
- [ ] Inspect infrastructure (irrigation, greenhouses, etc.)

**Legal/Financial**
- [ ] Review HOA CC&Rs (farm obligations)
- [ ] Analyze HOA financials (reserves, assessments)
- [ ] Confirm tax incentives / easements
- [ ] Title review + environmental reports

**Developer**
- [ ] Track record (prior agrihood experience?)
- [ ] Financial strength (ability to complete project)
- [ ] References from other developments
