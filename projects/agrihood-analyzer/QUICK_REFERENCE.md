## Investment Type Recommendations

### Conservative Investor
**Target**: Mature, established developments  
**Examples**: Agritopia (2000), Prairie Crossing (1994)  
**Score Range**: 65-80  
**Return Profile**: 4-6% appreciation, stable demand  

### Growth Investor
**Target**: Partial developments in high-growth metros  
**Examples**: Harvest Green (TX), Arden (FL)  
**Score Range**: 70-80  
**Return Profile**: 6-10% appreciation, higher volatility  

### Early-Stage / High Risk
**Target**: Planning/construction phase  
**Examples**: New Florida/Texas projects  
**Score Range**: 50-70  
**Return Profile**: 15-25% IRR potential, execution risk  

### Income Investor
**Target**: Established agrihoods for rental income  
**Examples**: Serenbe (Airbnb), Willowsford (long-term rental)  
**Score Range**: 75+  
**Return Profile**: 3-5% yield + appreciation

## Red Flags (Walk Away If...)

❌ **Water rights unclear or temporary** (agricultural operations = water critical)  
❌ **No professional farm staff hired** (DIY models fail frequently)  
❌ **HOA farm funding model undefined** (who pays for operations?)  
❌ **Developer has no agrihood experience** (specialty skill set required)  
❌ **Remote location >60 miles from metro** (limited buyer pool)  
❌ **No organic/sustainable certifications in wealthy markets** (buyers expect it)  
❌ **Cooperative farm model in large development** (coordination nightmare)  
❌ **Home prices <10% premium vs traditional** (agrihood value not resonating)  

---

## Green Flags (Strong Signals)

✅ **Professional farm staff in place** (reduced operational risk)  
✅ **Water rights secured permanently** (long-term viability)  
✅ **HOA funds farm via dedicated assessments** (sustainable model)  
✅ **Price premium 15-30%** (market values the agrihood component)  
✅ **Days on market <45** (strong demand)  
✅ **Developer track record in agrihoods** (Urban Land Institute members)  
✅ **Tax incentives + faster zoning** (government support)  
✅ **LEED/organic certifications** (sustainability credentials)  
✅ **Farm-to-table restaurant on-site** (revenue diversification)  
✅ **<30 miles from major metro** (accessible but rural feel)

## Next Steps by Experience Level

### New to Agrihoods
1. Tour 2-3 established agrihoods (Serenbe, Agritopia, Willowsford)
2. Read ULI's Agrihood Best Practices report
3. Talk to current residents (unfiltered feedback)
4. Start with secondary market purchase (lower risk)

### Experienced Real Estate Investor
1. Analyze recent comps in target agrihoods
2. Build detailed cash flow models (appreciation + rental scenarios)
3. Evaluate HOA financials (farm funding, reserves)
4. Consider early-stage development partnerships (higher returns)

### Angel Investor Mindset (Jack!)
1. Treat like startup due diligence: team, market, execution risk
2. Network with agrihood developers (deal flow)
3. Consider GP/LP structures (syndicate with other angels)
4. Evaluate agtech/services plays (picks and shovels strategy)
